CLINICAL GUIDELINES: TYPE 2 DIABETES MANAGEMENT
Version 3.2 | Acme Medical Reference | 2024 Edition
================================================================

SECTION 1: DIAGNOSIS AND SCREENING

Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) is diagnosed when any one of the following criteria is met:
- Fasting plasma glucose (FPG) ≥ 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) on two separate occasions
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) during a 75g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)
- HbA1c ≥ 6.5% (48 mmol/mol) confirmed by repeat testing
- Random plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL in a patient with classic hyperglycemia symptoms

Screening is recommended for all adults aged 35 and above, or younger adults with BMI ≥ 25 kg/m²
combined with one or more risk factors including: family history of T2DM, hypertension, dyslipidemia,
history of gestational diabetes, or polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

Prediabetes is defined as FPG between 100–125 mg/dL or HbA1c between 5.7–6.4%.
All prediabetic patients should be enrolled in a structured lifestyle intervention program.

SECTION 2: GLYCEMIC TARGETS

General HbA1c targets for non-pregnant adults:
- Target HbA1c: < 7.0% for most patients
- Less stringent target (< 8.0%): for elderly patients, those with hypoglycemia unawareness,
  limited life expectancy, or advanced complications
- More stringent target (< 6.5%): for younger patients with short disease duration,
  no cardiovascular disease, and low hypoglycemia risk

Fasting glucose target: 80–130 mg/dL
Postprandial glucose target (1–2 hours after meal): < 180 mg/dL

SECTION 3: FIRST-LINE PHARMACOTHERAPY

Metformin remains the preferred initial pharmacological agent for T2DM unless contraindicated.
Standard starting dose: 500 mg twice daily with meals, titrated up to 2000 mg/day over 4 weeks.

Contraindications for Metformin:
- eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m² (contraindicated); use with caution if eGFR 30–45
- Active hepatic disease or alcohol use disorder
- Iodinated contrast dye procedures (hold 48 hours before and after)

If HbA1c is ≥ 9.0% at diagnosis or if patient is symptomatic, consider:
- Dual therapy: Metformin + GLP-1 receptor agonist or SGLT2 inhibitor
- Insulin therapy if HbA1c ≥ 10% or patient has ketosis

SECTION 4: CARDIOVASCULAR AND RENAL PROTECTION

For patients with established cardiovascular disease (CVD) or high CVD risk:
- GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., semaglutide, liraglutide) are preferred add-on therapy
  due to cardiovascular outcome benefits
- SGLT2 inhibitors (e.g., empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) are recommended for patients
  with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF) or chronic kidney disease (CKD)

Patients with CKD (eGFR 25–60): SGLT2 inhibitors reduce progression of diabetic nephropathy.
Target blood pressure in T2DM with CKD: < 130/80 mmHg.
ACE inhibitors or ARBs are first-line antihypertensives in T2DM with proteinuria.

SECTION 5: LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS

All T2DM patients must receive Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT) delivered by a registered dietitian.
Recommended dietary approaches:
- Mediterranean diet or low-carbohydrate diet improves glycemic control
- Carbohydrate intake: individualized, typically 45–60% of total calories
- Sodium intake: < 2300 mg/day; further restriction to < 1500 mg/day if hypertension present
- Avoid sugar-sweetened beverages entirely

Physical activity recommendations:
- Minimum 150 minutes/week of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise
- Resistance training at least 2 days/week
- Sedentary time should be broken up every 30 minutes

Weight loss target: 5–10% body weight reduction improves glycemic control significantly.
Bariatric surgery should be considered for BMI ≥ 40 or BMI ≥ 35 with uncontrolled T2DM.

SECTION 6: MONITORING AND FOLLOW-UP

HbA1c testing: every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months.
Annual screening for:
- Diabetic retinopathy: dilated fundus examination
- Diabetic nephropathy: urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) + eGFR
- Peripheral neuropathy: 10g monofilament + vibration perception
- Lipid panel: LDL target < 100 mg/dL; < 70 mg/dL if CVD present
- Thyroid function: TSH annually in type 2 patients

Foot care: daily self-inspection, annual podiatry review.
Dental care: biannual review.

SECTION 7: HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT

Mild-to-moderate hypoglycemia:
- Rule of 15: 15g fast-acting carbohydrate; recheck glucose in 15 min.
Severe hypoglycemia:
- Glucagon 1mg IM/SC or intranasal glucagon 3mg; IV dextrose if available.
Emergency services should be contacted immediately.
